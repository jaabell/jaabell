**Benjamín Arrieta (2025)** - *Prediction and mitigation of early-age cracking in mass concrete due to hydration heat*
<!-- IMAGE: {static}/images/people/benjamin_arrieta.jpg -->
Se desarrolló una metodología para estimar y validar el comportamiento térmico y mecánico de elementos masivos de hormigón durante su proceso de curado, con especial énfasis en la curva de generación de calor asociada a la hidratación del cemento. Se diseñó un sistema experimental de bajo costo con un calorímetro instrumentado, y se implementó una estrategia de calibración inversa mediante simulaciones térmicas en OpenSees. Los resultados muestran que la metodología propuesta permite reproducir satisfactoriamente el perfil térmico experimental, y el uso de hielo como método de mitigación redujo las tensiones térmicas y el tamaño de grietas.

**José Luis Larenas (2024)** - *Development of Coupled Thermo-Mechanical Analysis in Mass Concrete using OpenSees*
<!-- IMAGE: {static}/images/people/jose_larenas.jpg -->
This thesis presents the development and implementation of coupled thermo-mechanical analysis capabilities in OpenSees for mass concrete applications. The work enables accurate prediction of thermal stresses and early-age cracking in large concrete elements. Non-linear analysis successfully predicted crack initiation and propagation at geometric discontinuities where stress concentrations are highest.

**Omar Oyarce (2024)** - *Comparative study of seismic simulation methodologies for structures threatened by the San Ramón fault*
<!-- IMAGE: {static}/images/people/omar_oyarce.jpg -->
This study evaluated how seismic metrics vary in structures threatened by the San Ramón Fault based on modeling type and seismic input complexity. Using variable fidelity models in OpenSees with STKO, results confirmed that seismic performance prediction is highly sensitive to input modeling, particularly near the fault. DRM models showed higher drift and base shear demands due to 3D seismic wave effects.

**Felipe Elgueta (2022)** - *Simulating fatigue behaviour of wind turbine blades through Isogeometric Analysis*
<!-- IMAGE: {static}/images/people/felipe_elgueta.jpg -->
<img src="https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/8tao844gblmajnkbimezp/elgueta.png?rlkey=vijo2uw0o5p9uz3drmtpd7i1d&raw=1" alt="It's me. Jose!" align="left" hspace="15" vspace="15" width=80%/>
In this thesis, experimental modal degradation data from a laboratory-tested wind-turbine blade were used with detailed numerical modeling to calibrate a progressive fatigue-damage constitutive model for fiber-reinforced laminated composites. The blade was modeled using isogeometric Kirchhoff-Love shell formulation and damage-based constitutive model implemented in OpenSees.
